London Borough of Camden - SharePoint Team Sites

Lead UX Developer on the rebranding project of team collaboration sites built on the SharePoint 2010 platform for over 6000 users. Primary objective was to create a system enabling users to store, manage and find their documents quickly and with ease.

Key deliverables

  • Ran workshops and conducted user interviews to define internal business labels for tagging documents using “term sets”. Designed taxonomy-driven navigation by enforcing users to tag their documents, aiding to refine document search
  • Produced wireframes and HTML prototypes for product owners and backend developers to illustrate complex user interactions, thus eliminating unnecessary development time by building it “right first time”
  • Designed a “look and feel” based on internal communication design guidelines to convey a sense of ownership for the business over an out-of-the box deployment
  • Encouraged the adoption of an online kanban tool to create user stories and conducted product backlog grooming session with product owner. This meant transparency and accountability amongst all team members for rapid changes.

  • Client: London Borough of Camden
  • Project: SharePoint Team Sites
  • Date: October 2012 - March 2013, August 2013 - December 2014
  • Service: Information Architecture, Interaction Design, Front End Development
