Process

The 3 primary devices we were aiming towards were mobile (320 x 480 pixels), tablet (< 768 pixels) and desktop (> 1024 x 768 pixels). Early prototypes identified what specific devices were capable of doing for example, how video would render across all platfroms. This would continue to evolve over the development phase.

High fidelity prototyping has major advantages over classical wireframes when dealing with responsive web design. It removes the need for constant documentation up until the final prototype has been approved. Users are able to see real world examples of how their design would render on a HTC Wildfire mobile device over Apple's Thunderbolt Cinema Display. It would also show the interactive nature and technical limitations of each device.

Seeing the design viewed on multiple devices raised key issues of hierarchical content, i.e. what content was relevant to the user at the time of using each device? The research conducted by the team to formulate customer journeys would assist in answering this, as well as meeting further discussions in aligning with the business goals.
