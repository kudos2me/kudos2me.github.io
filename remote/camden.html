London Borough of Camden - SharePoint Team Sites

Background

I was the lead UX Developer on the rebranding project of team collaboration sites built on the SharePoint 2010 platform for over 6000 users. Primary objective was to create a system enabling users to store, manage and find their documents quickly and with ease.

Process

I began the project by holding workshops and conducted user interviews with groups of 8 to 10 people of varying degrees in the organisation. These groups would help define a fixed set of internal labels used for tagging their documents. With these terms, I designed a taxonomy-driven navigation where users were required to tag all files uploaded into the system. This would also aid with faceted searches.

Having defined the functional specificaions, I then produced wireframes and HTML prototypes for product owners and backend developers to illustrate complex user interactions, thus eliminating unnecessary development time by building it "right first time"

A key requirement was to implement a custom look and feel that had to align with asthetics of the corporate intranet. With the intranet as a visual cue, I designed a corporate skin for our system using PhotoShop.

As part of our development methodology, I also encouraged the adoption of Trello, an online kanban tool used to create user stories and conducted product backlog grooming session with product owner. This meant transparency and accountability amongst all team members for rapid changes.
